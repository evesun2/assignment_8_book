Chapter 1: Guide to the World Cup¶

The FIFA World Cup is a soccer or football competition held every four years. The tournament hosts 32 countries. This year’s World Cup tournament is in Qatar. The logo (Fig. 1) was designed by a studio in Lisbon, Portugal [noab].

Fig. 1 The logo to this year’s World Cup.¶

One of the countries participating this year is Canada (Fig. 2). Canada’s first World Cup match is against Belgium. Belgium came third in the last World Cup in 2018.

Fig. 2 The Canadian flag.¶

This is Canada’s first time qualifying since 1984.

Gambling¶

One of the most popular things to do during the World Cup is betting on who will win [noa22].

Several websites post a Odds number like -500 but what does that actually mean in percent probability [noaa].

To convert the odd number to winnings, you would use the following formula:

if American number odds is negative:

(1)¶\[winnings = \frac{AmntBet}{100}|odds|\]

if American odds number is positive:

(2)¶\[winnings = \frac{AmntBet}{odds}100\]

For example, if the odds are -200 and you bet 100 dollars, your winnings are \(frac{100}{100}*|-200|\) for a total of 200 dollars according to equation (1). If the odds were above 0, you would need to use equation (2)[noaa].

Note

Betting against the odds (>0) yields greater winnings if the country you bet on wins.

To convert the odds to a %probability of winning, you would use the following formula:

(3)¶\[probability of winning = \frac{AmtWin-AmtBet}{AmtWin}\]

Therefore, if you bet 100 dollars on a -500 odds team, the odds would be \( frac{500-100}{500} = 80% \)according to equation (3).

Danger

Betting is at your own risk, if the team you betted on loses, you will lose the amount you wagered.
